# The Reality of the Universe in the Brain

Hello, dear thinking, questioning, and searching brains.

Today marks the 50th anniversary of September 13, 1963 for me. It is the date on which I entered these subjects.

Who am I? What is this existence? What is the truth of existence and of myself? I was at the age and time of searching for the answers to these questions. I believed in nothing. I had to start somewhere, and I started on this date.

Throughout this process, there was no subject I did not examine, question, or research, and whatever practices were required by this knowledge, I lived through all of them within myself.

The things I always wondered about: what is the truth of existence? What is the thing I call "I"? At what level does life continue? Is there something beyond death or not? What is my place in the universe? What are the realms? Where do the people who die go? Is there anyone in space? Do they come and go? I researched all of these.

And now I would like to share with you some of my findings in the company of the most recent scientific frameworks. I hope it will be of benefit.

Of course we will carry out this examination with our brains. The brain is the mother of all subjects. Whatever subject you take up, your sole key to solving that subject is the brain. Therefore, without knowing the brain, no one can get anywhere. One can only deceive oneself in one's own world of imagination.

The starting point of the matter, my friends, is to understand the brain well. The event begins in the brain and ends in the brain. What we call the human being consists entirely of the brain. But not the flesh-brain you know. You all generally know what you define as the flesh-brain — that walnut-shaped piece of flesh that comes out when you cut the skull in half. Is it really a flesh-brain? Is the thing you call "my brain" that flesh-brain?

Let me try to explain this to you in the following way. I acquired a magic glasses. According to the controls on these glasses, they can magnify one hundred thousand times, one million times, one hundred million times, one billion times. Now let me put on these magic glasses and try to tell you what I see through them.

My friends, here we have a brain. This is the flesh-brain we know. As we see it, the thing we define as a flesh-brain. We say this brain thinks, sees, feels, and perceives — but of course it does all of this through organs such as the eyes and ears.

Now my magic glasses will magnify this. When magnified, this flesh-brain suddenly turned into a neuron-brain. Yes, a neuron-brain consisting of nerve cells, neurons, moving within electrical flows, signals connecting to one another. I was seeing this with the naked eye as a flesh-brain, but it turns out it is a neuron-brain. A mass of neurons.

Well then, magic glasses, please magnify this further. The neurons transformed into atoms. I am now inside the atoms, and my body is a structure consisting entirely of atoms.

Just a short while ago, when I was looking with my eye, I was seeing this as a flesh-and-bone body, but now I see it as a body consisting entirely of atoms.

Magic, please magnify this further. Existence became a Quantum Brain. And I lost my body as well. I am a being in this environment consisting only of a thought, only of information. At the quantum level, I have neither a body nor any other structure. I live as a being consisting only of thought, only of knowledge, only of data.

Well then, magic glasses, thank you. Yes, a truth has emerged. What we call the material dimension is nothing other than what we call the quantum dimension. What we call the quantum dimension is nothing other than what we call the material dimension. There is no such thing as a material dimension, an atomic dimension, and a quantum dimension as separate things. A single structure, a single body, a single existence.

Whether you call it matter, or cellular, or atomic, or quantum — there is only one single existing structure. That single structure is a structure consisting of data, of knowledge, of information.

Here, what we call the brain — formed from the knowledge that has entered it, that constitutes it — perceives, and names existence by evaluating it according to the data it already holds. Quantum information is evaluated by the quantum brain. But according to the information that comes to the brain.

Therefore, existence is a single whole. Because existence is a single wholeness, there are no parallel universes, there are no non-parallel universes, and there are no other dimensions. All of it is a single existence, a single body, a single structure.

But the brain, according to the data carried by the rays and waves that reach it and that it perceives, assigns names and definitions, and according to those definitions creates its own world within itself.

Therefore — oh, pardon me — where did those who departed from us, those who died, go? They went nowhere. They are wherever they are.

Now the question — you will say: with what we call death, this thing we call the flesh-brain will disintegrate. The flesh-brain may disintegrate. But there is no such thing as the disintegration of the flesh-brain, because the truth of the flesh-brain is the quantum brain. The brain that exists in the quantum brain right now — the perceiving, database-possessing brain — is preserved exactly as it is. Therefore there is no such thing as death. There is no such thing as annihilation. Every brain, every consciousness, continues its life endlessly. And they are among us.

Extraterrestrials? When you speak of extraterrestrials, when you can no longer see them, all of us are together within this single structure. But because the brain cannot evaluate the waves coming from there, we cannot decipher them.

Now, the data that comes to this brain — ah, the eye, my pupil. My pupil imprisons me. While my pupil shows me all of this, it also confines me to all of this, leading me to make false judgments and to deny the countless things that exist.

The brain, evaluating the data in the wavelengths between four ten-thousandths and seven ten-thousandths of a centimeter that the eye transmits to it, speaks of what it sees.

So with what do I see the universe? With my pupil. Those telescopes that show us the infinite universe — they all transmit only the range between four and seven ten-thousandths to my brain, through my pupil.

Therefore, the infinite wavelengths remaining beside that four ten-thousandths, the infinite wavelengths remaining beside the seven ten-thousandths, all the data, knowledge, and structure contained within these — what becomes of them? I do not know.

The eye was formed, was created, solely to see what lies within this range. As for the countless wavelengths remaining in the other ranges, they are certainly being transmitted to brains by eyes that perceive them as well. Therefore, there are many kinds of brains, there are brains, that will perceive the beings possessing the knowledge contained in those wavelengths.

In other words, existence is knowledge, data — and the data-brains, the quantum brains, that evaluate that knowledge and data.

Now, what is my idea of the universe? Or yours? There is the solar system we see in pictures and films, the Milky Way galaxy to which the solar system belongs — it is one shore, on that shore there are 30 local island galaxies, and together with those local island galaxies, this galaxy is located somewhere within a large group of galaxies, and there Andromeda is approaching our Milky Way, and there is a newly identified galaxy, and we are moving toward it. And billions of galaxies. Billions of galaxies. We say we see the whole universe. How absurd.

The universe we see is the universe of data that the wavelengths between four and seven ten-thousandths of a centimeter transmit to our brains through our eyes. We are entirely unaware of what the real universe is.

Let me try to explain with a simple example. There is a steel wall in front of you. We created a three-finger-wide gap in it. We placed an elephant behind it. That gap shows exactly the belly, the navel of the elephant. You look through that gap, and based on what you see, you say: an elephant is something like a cube. But if we were to create completely different gaps over there and could see through them as well, we would see something entirely different when it comes to the elephant. With its trunk, its thin tail, its pillar-like legs, and its shovel-shaped ears.

Now this is how our view of the universe is. Just as we see the elephant's belly through that notch, through what the wavelengths between four and seven reflect to us, we imagine a universe made up of galaxies like this. If we could perceive through wavelengths outside of this range, this universe would change shape as it is, and we would not be speaking of a universe like this.

All the countless voids between those galaxies — white matter, dark matter, black holes, white holes — what will become of these, where will they go, or in what way will we perceive them?

In those places too, countless beings or structures exist. The perceptual centers that will perceive these structures exist in brains. Consciousness preserves its existence exactly, and nothing is lost in the brain; it all remains present, and experience continues.

And while we think we are living in the universe, outside, in the world — in reality there is yet another truth we know today.

So where does all of this that we see take place? Do I actually see this entire universe outside? This is the greatest matter of the eye — or more accurately, of the brain.
